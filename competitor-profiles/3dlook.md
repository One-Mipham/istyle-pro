## Positioning & Messaging

**Primary value proposition**: Accurate body measurements from just 2 smartphone photos — replace measuring tapes, sizing charts, and manual measurements with AI-driven 3D body models.

**Target audience**: Apparel retailers (size recommendation), uniform/workwear companies (remote fitting), and health/fitness platforms (body tracking). Strictly B2B.

**Positioning angle**: "The measurement layer" — they don't do virtual try-on themselves; they provide the precise body data that makes try-on and size recommendation possible.

**Key messaging themes**:
- Accuracy first: sub-centimeter measurement precision
- Reduce returns through better sizing
- Replace physical measurement with AI
- Enterprise-ready: API + SDK + dashboard

## Product & Features

### Core capabilities
- **YourFit**: 2-photo → 80+ body measurements + 3D body model in under 30 seconds
- **SizeCheck**: Size recommendation widget for e-commerce product pages
- **BodyShape**: 3D avatar generation from measurements for virtual try-on partners
- **Mobile SDK**: Embed body scanning into partner apps
- **API**: Integrate measurement data into existing retail systems

### Notable differentiators
- Measurement accuracy is the core differentiator — they claim <1cm error
- Not a virtual try-on app per se — they're the measurement infrastructure layer
- Focus on solving the "what size am I?" problem, not the "how do I look?" problem

### Product direction signals
- Expanding into health/fitness vertical
- Uniform and workwear B2B is a growing segment
- Likely building toward being acquired by a larger retail tech platform

## Strengths & Weaknesses

### Strengths
- Best-in-class AI body measurement accuracy from simple photos
- Solves a real, measurable problem (returns cost retailers billions)
- Fills a critical gap in the VTO pipeline — precise measurements improve try-on realism
- Dual application: retail + health/fitness

### Weaknesses
- **Not a virtual try-on company** — they're a measurement tool. Users don't see how clothes look on them
- No visual/style component at all — purely functional, zero emotional engagement
- No consumer-facing product — entirely dependent on retailer adoption
- Niche B2B positioning limits growth and brand recognition
- Could be commoditized — Google/Apple could build body measurement into their camera APIs

## Competitive Implications for iStyle Pro

**Where they're strong vs. us**: Measurement precision. If iStyle Pro's AI try-on looks unrealistic because of poor body mapping, users will notice. 3DLOOK has 10 years of measurement ML data.

**Where we're strong vs. them**: iStyle Pro is a **visual and emotional** experience — we show how you *look*, not just tell you your measurements. We have direct consumer relationship. We do the full job: measure → style → visualize.

**Opportunities**: iStyle Pro could potentially **partner with or integrate 3DLOOK** for the measurement layer, rather than building it from scratch. Their API/SDK is designed for exactly this. This would accelerate our accuracy while we focus on the AI generation + style curation.

**Threats**: Low threat. They're infrastructure, we're application. Complementary rather than competitive. The threat would be if a virtual try-on competitor (like DRESSX) acquires them for exclusive access.
